Support

Real human on the other end

Nouri is built and supported by one person. If something is broken, confusing, or missing, email brianchan97hk@gmail.com and you'll get a reply — usually within a day or two.

For urgent allergen concerns If you experience an allergic reaction, seek medical help immediately — do not rely on Nouri for emergency decisions. Always verify allergens directly on product packaging.

§ 01Frequently asked

How accurate is Nouri's allergen detection?

Nouri's analysis is generated by an AI model that reads the photo you take of the ingredient list. It's useful as a second pair of eyes, but it can miss ingredients, misread faint text, or misinterpret complex labels. If you have a diagnosed allergy, always read the physical label and follow your doctor's guidance. Nouri is not a medical device.

Do you see the photos I scan?

No. Scan images are sent to Google's Gemini AI for analysis and are not retained by us afterwards. Only the structured result (product name, ingredients, macros, allergen flags) is saved to your account so features like pantry and history can work.

How do I cancel Nouri Plus?

Cancellations are managed through your Google Play or Apple account settings — the same place you manage any other subscription on your device. Cancellation takes effect at the end of the current billing period.

Can I delete my account?

Yes — open the app, go to Profile → § Danger zone → Delete account. Your account and all associated data are removed immediately. If you can't sign in, email brianchan97hk@gmail.com from the address linked to your account. Full details at delete-account.

Why does Nouri need camera access?

To scan nutrition labels. The camera is only used while you are actively on the scan screen — we do not watch, record, or use the camera in the background.

When is the iPhone version coming?

We're launching on Android first. iPhone support is on the roadmap — join the waitlist and we'll let you know when it's ready.

I have a bug or a feature request.

Wonderful — those are the most useful emails. Include your device model, Android version, and a description of what happened. Screenshots help a lot.
